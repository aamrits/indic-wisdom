# Arjuna

Name: *Arjuna*  
Pronunciation: *Ar-ju-na*  
Meaning: *The Pure One*  
Title: *Son of Indra. Peerless archer, chosen instrument of divine wisdom*  

Arjuna, the third of the five Pandavas, was born to Queen *Kunti* through the divine invocation of *Indra*. Arjuna was one of the most complex characters with his unmatched archery, discipline and devotion to Krishna.

Arjuna represents the sincere seeker. Though invincible in battle, Arjuna was known for his humility and willingness to seek guidance. *Krishna* chose to give the wisdom of the *Bhagavad Gita* to him.
Arjuna is known by ten names, i.e., *Arjuna, Phalguna, Jishnu, Kiriti,  Shvetavahana, Bibhatsu, Vijaya, Krishna, Savyasachi and  Dhananjaya*.

---

## Birth and Lineage

Kunti, in her childhood, had served the great sage *Durvasa* with utmost reverence and honour. Satisfied with her duty and service, Durvasa gave her a mantra and said that she could obtain a son from whichever god she would invoke. 

Due to the curse of a rishi, Kunti’s husband *Pandu* couldn’t obtain a child in the normal way for fear of death. When Pandu came to know about Kunti’s boon, he wished for three sons. Kunti gave the mantra to her co-wife Madri, and she had two sons. In this way, Pandu had five sons, known as *Pandavas*.

Pandu wished for a child who would be the best of three worlds. He wished for a son by *Indra*, the king and best of the gods. He stood on one leg and performed severe austerities in mind, deeds and speech. Kunti observed a sacred vow for one year.  

Indra was pleased and blessed them with a son, known as *Arjuna*. As he was born, *the gandharvas, the apsaras, the saptarishis, the maharshis, the adityas, the rudras, the ashvins, and the serpents* came and praised Arjuna. A divine voice declared that Arjuna would be equal to *Vishnu* and *Parashurama* in valour, unite all the kingdoms, and increase the prosperity of the *Kurus*.

Arjuna and Krishna were ancient sages, *Nara* and *Narayana*, and they were invincible in every way.

---

## Major Victories

- While going to Panchala to attend Droupadi’s *svayamvara*, Arjuna defeated a *gandharva* named *Chitraratha*. Later, the gandharva gave the knowledge of *chakshushi*, by which Arjuna could see whatever he wished to see in the three worlds.

- Arjuna fought and defeated Karna in Droupadi’s svayamvara.

- Before the *Rajasuya* sacrifice could be conducted, Arjuna, Bhima, Nakula and Sahadeva went and defeated kings through force and reconciliation. Arjuna went to the north and defeated many powerful kings.

- During exile, Arjuna defeated the entire army of *Nivatakavachas* single-handedly and brought the city of Hiranyapura, where the *Poulama and Kalakeya* danavas resided, to his subjugation.

- During exile, Arjuna fought Karna (kills his younger brother), Kripa, Drona, Ashvatthama, Duryodhana and Bhisma. At last, he defeated everyone with his divine *Aindra* weapon.

---

## Major Episodes

- *Training under Drona*: Arjuna excelled beyond all princes, mastering advanced astras, including the *Brahmastra*.

- *Winning Droupadi*: At her svayamvara, Arjuna struck the rotating target, securing Droupadi’s hand in marriage.

- *Arjuna’s other wives*: To save the brahmanas, Arjuna entered Yudhishthira’s room while he was with Droupadi. For this, Arjuna observed *brahmacharya* and lived in the forest for 12 years. However, he was forced to marry *Ulupi* (a naga princess). He also fell in love with *Chitrangada* and *Subhadra* and married them. Only Subhadra stayed with Droupadi in Indraprastha.

- *Burning of the Khandava forest*: Agni came and asked for Arjuna and Krishna's help in burning the Khandava forest. To accomplish this difficult task, *Agni* gave Arjuna the *Gandiva bow*, 2 inexhaustible quivers and a chariot bearing the flag of a monkey. Krishna also obtained the *Sudarshana chakra* and *Koumadaki club*. Apart from Ashvasena (*Takshaka’s* son), the demon *Maya* and four birds (peacocks), everything was destroyed and consumed by the great fire. Later, Maya built the great assembly hall of Indraprastha, which was the best among the three worlds.

- *Exile*: During exile, Arjuna stayed with Indra for five years. Indra even made Arjuna sit in the divine throne of Indra. After severe penance, Shiva gave Arjuna the celestial weapon known as the *pashupata* weapon. Varuna, Kubera, and Yama also gave their weapons to Arjuna. He also learned singing and dancing from *Chitrasena*.

---

## Role in Mahabharata war

- Arjuna was the only one to counter Bhisma on multiple occasions. On the ninth day, he was angry, and his arrows were flowing like a stream. Bhima was pierced all over his body and fell on a bed of arrows. Arjuna create a stream of water (like *amrita*) to quench his thirst.

- There was an army known as the *Samshaptakas*, who swore an oath to never return from the battlefield without killing Arjuna. While Arjuna was fighting with them, he couldn’t protect his son *Abhimanyu*. Eventually, Arjuna killed all of them. 

- Arjuna used the Indra weapon multiple times to restrain the Kauravas. He killed the powerful *Bhagadatta*, Shakuni’s brothers, Karna’s brothers and sons, Dandadhara and Danda from Magadha, Ugrayudha, Satyasena, Satyakriti, Mitradeva, Shrutanjaya, Soushruti, Chitrasena, Mitravarma, the Trigartas, many Kambojas, the Yavanas and the Shakas.

- When Drona was killed, Arjuna was ashamed and protested. He said that they had killed their preceptor using *adharma*. Similarly, when Bhurishrava was killed by Satyaki, Arjuna protested. 

- When Ashvatthama invoked the *Narayana* weapon, only Bhima refused to surrender. It was because of Arjuna, who created a shield around him, that Bhima was protected.

- Ashvatthama also invoked the *Agneya* weapon, which returned without killing Arjuna and Krishna, and killed only one akshouhini. With disbelief, Ashvatthama ran away from the field. Later, Sage Vyasa revealed that Arjuna and Krishna were, in fact, the ancient sages *Nara and Narayana*, and Shiva had blessed them. They couldn’t be killed in battle.

- There were times when Arjuna was defeated and fell unconscious in the battle. However, each time he bounced back and killed his opponent.

- Only Arjuna knew how to use and withdraw the powerful *brahmashira* weapon.

---

## Role in Mahabharata war - The 14th day

- Arjuna performed the most impossible feat on the 14th day, showing why he was the most terrible warrior in the Mahabharata. To avenge Abhimanyu’s death, Arjuna destroyed *7 akshauhinis* on a single day with a single chariot driven by Krishna.

- *Drona* constructed the vyuha partly as a *chakra* and partly as a *sakata*. It was 12 *govyutis* long and 4 govyutis wide. At the rear, there was a core vyuha in the form of a lotus. In the middle of the lotus, there was a needle-like stalk. *Jayadratha* was present in the midst of that, protected by Karna on the right, *Ashvatthama* on the left, *Kripa, Vrishasena, Shala and Shalya* on the rear and *Somadatta’s son* at the forefront. Drona said even the gods couldn’t have broken the formation.

- Arjuna fought with Drona and was restrained. However, Krishna drove the chariot so fast and took Arjuna away from Drona’s chariot. Drona could do nothing but watch.

- Arjuna would create a small passage so that the chariot would pass. Then Krishna, with his expert and swift skill, would glide the chariot inside that small passage. Thus, Arjuna gradually and steadily entered the terrible Kuru army formation. 

- Apart from *Arjuna*, only *Bhima* and *Satyaki* penetrated the Kuru army formation constructed by Drona.

- When Krishna said the horses were tired, Arjuna, in the middle of the war, constructed a house of arrows and created a pond. He fought the entire Kuru army on the ground. Nobody could touch him. Meanwhile, Krishna took care of the horses and made them ready to fight. Seeing this, Kuru warriors were ashamed of their strength and being kshatriyas.

- Finally, when Arjuna saw the head of Jayadratha, he aimed an arrow that cut off Jayadratha’s head. Then, using a series of arrows, Arjuna carried Jayadratha’s head and landed it on the lap of Jayadratha’s father, *killing both Jayadratha and his father, Vriddhakshatra*.

## Footnotes

- *Arjuna*: One who performs pure deeds.

- *Kunti*: Mother of Yudhishthira. The husband of Kunti is Pandu.

- *Indra*: Indra is a title given to the king of the gods. Indra is also referred to as the god of rain. The name of Indra is Shakra.

- *Krishna*: The eighth avatar of Vishnu. He is also said to be the ancient sage Narayana. Friend of the Pandavas, Arjuna, in particular. Kunti, mother of the Pandavas, was the paternal aunt of Krishna.

- *Bhagavad Gita*: The celestial song given by God (Krishna) to Arjuna about knowing one’s dharma. It also talks about yoga, liberation and the duty (eternal dharma) of every man.

- *Phalguna*: One who is born in the slopes of the Himalayas when Purva and Uttara Phalguni nakshatras are visible.

- *Jishnu*: One who is difficult to repress.

- *Kiriti*: Arjuna wore Indra’s diadem on his head.

- *Shvetsvahana*: In battle, only white steeds are yoked to his chariot.

- *Bibhatsu*: One who has never committed a terrible act in battle.

- *Vijaya*: One who never returns without vanquishing his enemies.

- *Krishna*: One with a dark complexion.

- *Savyasachi*: One who can fight with both hands.

- *Dhananjaya*: One who has conquered all the countries and obtained wealth, one who stands amidst riches.

- *Durvasa*: A great and angry sage, often known as a part of Shiva.

- *Pandu*: The husband of Kunti, the young brother of Dhritarashtra.

- *Gandharvas, apsaras, saptarishis, adityas, rudras, ashvins, serpents*: Gandharvas are celestial male singers, ruled by Kubera. Apsaras are beautiful celestial female angels, singing and dancing in Indra’s court. Saptarishis are the seven celestial sages or seers. Adityas are born from Aditi, and Sage Kashyapa is their father. Among 33 gods present in our scriptures, there are 12 Adityas, 11 Rudras, 8 Vasus, and 2 Ashvins. All the serpents are born from Kadru, their father being the same Kashyapa.

- *Vishnu*: One of the three energies (Trinity) of the cosmos. Brahma being the creator, Shiva being the destroyer, Vishnu is known to be the preserver or protector.

- *Parashurama*: The son of Sage Jamadagni and the sixth incarnation of Vishnu. He destroyed all the evil and arrogant kshatriyas 21 times from the face of the earth. He is also known as a teacher to Bhisma, Drona, and Karna in the Mahabharata.

- *Nara and Narayana*: The ancient sages, very powerful and ancient. As per Sage Vyasa and many others, Arjuna and Krishna were the ancient sages Nara and Narayana. 

- *Svayamvara*: A process where a bride (mostly a princess) chooses her husband from a group of suitors. Normally, the husband accomplishes a difficult task in front of everyone. Similarly, in *viryashulka*, a man displays extraordinary valour, and the bride is given to such a man by the bride’s father. The bride doesn’t have a choice.

- *Chakshushi*: Knowledge through a man can choose to see anything in the three worlds if he wishes to see. Arjuna obtained this knowledge from Chitraratha.

- *Rajasuya Sacrifice*: A prestigious Vedic consecration ceremony performed by kings to establish themselves as an emperor or *king of kings*. Yudhishthira demonstrated absolute sovereignty through the *digvijaya* (victory in all directions) conducted by his brothers.

- *Nivatakavachas*: Those who have impenetrable armours. They are referred to as demons, descendants of Prahlada. Poulama and Kalakeya are among them.

- *Aindra, Agneya, Rudra, Brahmastra, Brahmashira, Pashupata*: Aindra is Indra’s weapon. Agneya is a weapon of fire; it emanates immense fire. Brahmastra and Brahmashira are both Brahma’s weapons. Rudra and Pashupata are Shiva’s weapons. 

- *Brahmacharya*: A person who undertakes celibacy for a certain period of time.

- *Ulupi*: Naga princess and second wife of Arjuna. When Arjuna was observing brahmacharya, Ulupi saw him, got attracted and captured him. She insisted on marrying him, or else she would give her life. Hesitatingly, Arjuna married her. Ulupi granted Arjuna a boon that he would be capable of vanquishing all amphibious creatures. Iravan was their son,

- *Chitrangada*: Princess of Manipura and third wife of Arjuna. On seeing her, Arjuna fell in love. But her father put a condition that their child would be the future heir of Manipura, and Chitrangada would not leave the kingdom, as she was the Putrika (successor). Arjuna stayed in Manipura for 3 years, and they had a son, Babhruvahana.

- *Subhadra*: Krishna’s sister and Arjuna’s fourth wife. Subhadra eloped with Arjuna, and Krishna comforted his clan that the marriage was as per dharma. Their son Abhimanyu was a great warrior even as a child. His son, Parikshit, became the king of Hastinapura after the Pandavas decided to go to heaven after hearing of  Krishna’s death.

- *Gandiva bow*: Agni wanted to destroy the Khandava forest for a long time, as Takshaka, the serpent, was living there. Indra protected him. Disguised as a brahmana, Agni asked Arjuna and Krishna to burn the forest. Agni gave the Gandiva bow for that purpose.

- *Sudarsana Chakra, Koumadaki club*: Agni also gave Krishna the Sudarsana Chakra and Koumadaki club for burning Khadava forest.

- *Takshaka*: A terrible snake who wished to destroy the Pandavas. Indra considered him a friend and protected him. Though Takshaka couldn’t harm the Pandavas, he eventually killed Parikshit. Janamejaya, Parikshit’s son, got to know this and conducted a snake sacrifice where most of the snakes were destroyed. Ashtika, the child sage, finally stopped it. In this snake sacrifice, Vaishampayana, disciple of Sage Vyasa, narrated the Mahabharata for the first time.

- *Maya*: Maya is a demon mentioned in the Ramayana, also. In the Mahabharata, he built the extraordinary assembly hall of Indraprastha.

- *Akshauhini*: A measurement of an army. It consists of 21870 elephants, 21870 chariots, 65610 horses, and 109350 foot soldiers (Total 218,700 warriors).

- *Govyuti*: It is a measure of distance, i.e., its the distance from which a cow's bellow can be heard. A govyuti is thus roughly 4 miles (some texts suggest 2 miles). 2000 bows make 1 govyuti and 4 govyuti make up a single yojana. 

- *Drona*: The teacher of the Pandavas and Kauravas. His death was not possible, and hence, he was killed by unfair means by Dhristadyumna. Drona married Kripacharya’s sister, Kripi, and Ashvatthama was their son.

- *Jayadratha*: King of Sindhu, Duryodhana’s sister Duhsala married him. He tried to abduct Droupadi, but was saved by the Pandavas. Bhima didn’t kill him but shaved his head as humiliation. Arjuna killed him by placing his severed head on the lap of his father, Vriddhakshatra.

- *Shalya*: Ruler of Madra, brother to Madri and maternal uncle to Nakula and Sahadeva and the Pandavas. 

- *Somadatta’s son*: Bhurishrava was the primary son of Somadatta. He was killed unfairly by Satyaki in the Mahabharata war.

- *Satyaki*: Satyaki was a powerful warrior of the Vrishni lineage. Arjuna taught him archery, and he was one of the invincible warriors in the Mahabharata. 

- *Amrita*: nectar, elixir of life.

- *Samshaptakas*: The group of soldiers who had taken an oath to kill Arjuna or die trying on the battlefield.

- *Abhimanyu*: Son of Arjuna and Subhadra. Uttara was his wife, and Parikshit was his son who became the ruler.

- *Bhagadatta*: Son of Narakasura, Bhagadatta was the ruler of Pratyaghosa and a great friend of Indra. However, in the Mahabharata, he fought on the Kauravas' side and was eventually killed by Arjuna.
