# Puranas

Name: *Purānas*  
Pronunciation: *Puraanas*  
Meaning: *The Terrible / The Formidable One*  
Title: *Mighty son of Vayu (the wind god)*  

The word Purana means old. The Puranas were composed by *Krishna Dvaipayana Vedavyasa* (the sage’s name is Krishna Dvaipayana, and his title is Vedavyasa). It is said that he composed the Puranas after writing the Mahabharata.
 
The Vayu, Brahmanda and Vishnu puranas tell us how the original purana came into existence. Krishna Dvaipayana composed a single Veda and divided it into four: the *Rigveda, Samaveda, Yajurveda, and Atharvaveda*. Then he entrusted them to his 4 disciples: *Paila, Vaisampayana, Jaimini, and Sumantu*. 

After composing the Vedas and the Mahabharata, he compiled a Purana from all the tales, anecdotes, songs, and lore that had come down through the ages. From remote times, *Sutas* (bards, minstrels) have preserved the genealogies of gods, rishis, and kings, as well as traditions, through tales, ballads, and songs (as narrated in the Vayu and Padma Puranas). That was their duty and livelihood. Hence, Vedavyasa taught the Purana to his fifth disciple, *Suta Romaharshana* (or Lomaharsana). 

Romaharshana taught it to his son *Ugrasrava*. Also known as *Sauti*, Ugrasrava is the reciter of the Puranas.

---

## Who narrated the Puranas

There was a great sage, *Sounaka*, who was much accomplished. He taught and fed 10,000 students. He was performing a sacrifice that lasted for 12 years in *Naimisharanya* (Naimisha forest). 

Ugrashrava, the son of Lomaharshana and the son of a suta, arrived at the hermitage of Naimisharanya. He was also addressed by the title Sauti, meaning the son of a suta. He had attended the great snake sacrifice of *King Janamejaya*, the son of *Parikshit*. In that sacrifice, he heard the wonderful and sacred stories composed by Krishna Dvaipayana and recited by his disciple, Vaishampayana. 

After listening to those stories of the Mahabharata, Ugrashrava went to many places of pilgrimage and sacred waters. He went to the place known as *Samantapanchaka*, where the great battle was fought between the Kurus and the Pandavas, known as *Mahabharata*.

Having come to Naimisharanya from there, Ugrashrava bowed to the sages with humility. The sages, having performed sacred rites and meditation, had become pure, and they were shining like the fire of the Sun. Ugrashrava asked them which stories they would like to hear. He offered to narrate the stories of the Puranas, the source of dharma and artha, as well as the history of kings, sages, and great souls.

The sages wished to hear that text which Vaishampayana recited in the great sacrifice of King Janamejaya. They wished to hear that text which was holy and which drives away fear of sin. They wished to hear the Bharata, which was recited at Janamejaya’s sacrifice by Vaishampayana.

Hence, Ugrasrava first narrated the Mahabharata and then the Puranas to the sages residing in the Naimisharanya.

## Classification of Maha-puranas

- Puranas are classified based on *Brahma, Vishnu and Shiva*.

| Brahma | Vishnu | Shiva |
|------|------|-------|
| Brahma, Brahmanda, Brahmavaivarta, Markandeya | Bhagavata, Garuda, Kurma, Matsya, Narada, Padma, Vamana, Varah, Vishnu | Agni, Linga, Skanda, Shiva, Vayu |

- Puranas are classified based on qualities, i.e., *Sattva, Rajas and Tamas*.

| Sattva | Rajas | Tamas |
|------|------|-------|
| Bhagavata, Garuda, Narada, Padma, Varah, Vishnu | Bhavishya, Brahma, Brahmanda, Brahmavaivarta, Markandeya, Vamana | Agni, Kurma, Linga, Matsya, Skanda, Shiva |
