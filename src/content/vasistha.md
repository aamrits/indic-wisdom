# Sage Vasistha

Pronunciation: *Va-sish-tha*  
Meaning: *most excellent* 

Sage *Vasistha* is one of the foremost Rishis of the Vedic tradition. He is referred as one of the saptarishis (seven sages). He is the mentals son of *Brahma*. 

His presence across Itihāsa and philosophical literature is vast. He is the most respected sage and is a brahmarshi (someone who has realized *brahman*) or a self-realized person.

---

## Birth and Lineage

Vasistha is traditionally regarded as a mental son of *Brahma*.

He is counted among the *Sapta Rishis* and is married to *Arundhati*, who is herself revered as an ideal wife.

---

## Key Narratives

- The most important event is his conflict between Sage *Vishwamitra*. With his spiritual power, Vasistha defeats the entire army of Vishwamitra. He also defeats Vishwamitra with all his divine weapons he got from the gods. Finally, after long penance, Vasistha accepts Vishwamitra as a *brahmarshi*.

- Sage Vasistha impacts the supreme wisdom to Rama when he was a child. That knowledge is compiled as *Yoga Vasistha*.

- Sage Vasistha was the royal priest of the *Ikshvaku* dynasty, i.e. the solar dynasty. He was basically the king-maker and ensured the kings followed the path of *dharma*. *Rama*, his father *Dasaratha* belonged to Ikshvaku dynasty and  they paid homage to Vasistha.

## Footnotes

- *Brahma*: The creator aspect of the cosmic trinity in Hindu philosophy. He is associated with creation, knowledge, and the unfolding of the universe.

- *Brahman*: The ultimate, infinite reality that underlies all existence. It is formless, eternal, and beyond attributes, known through direct realization rather than perception.

- *Sapta rishis*: The seven primordial sages who preserved and transmitted Vedic knowledge. They represent cosmic wisdom and the continuity of spiritual lineage across ages.

- *Arundhati*: She is the exemplary wife of Sage Vasistha, revered for her chastity, wisdom, and devotion. She symbolizes ideal marital harmony and moral steadfastness.

- *Vishwamitra*: A sage who attained Brahmarshi status through intense tapas. In the earlier days, he was a king and intensely rivaled Sage Vasistha. However, after realizing the Brahman, he respected Vasistha. Vishwamitra is also one of the saptarishis.

- *Brahmarshi*: A sage who has realized Brahman through supreme knowledge and austerity. This title denotes the highest spiritual attainment among rishis.

- *Yoga Vasistha*: A philosophical dialogue between Sage Vasistha and Rama on the nature of reality and liberation. It presents Advaita teachings through stories, metaphors, and deep inquiry.

- *Ikshvaku*: He is the son of Manu (the first man. He was the son of Surya (Sun) and from him the term manushya is derived) and also the first king of Solar dynasty. He is regarded as the ancestral progenitor of many righteous rulers, including Lord Rama, Raghu, Kakutshta, Sagara, Prithu, Bhagiratha, etc.

- *Dharma*: Dharma is the principle that upholds order, righteousness, and harmony in life and the cosmos. It varies by context, role, and stage of existence.

- *Rama*: The seven avatar of Lord Vishnu. He took human form to kill Ravana, the powerful rakshasa. He set the highest standards for moral values and ideal conduct.That is the reason Sage Valmiki wrote the book Ramayana, the progression of Rama.

- *Dasaratha*: Father of Rama, King of Ayodhya.

- *Moksha*: It means liberation from the cycle of birth and death. It is attained through self-knowledge and the realization of one’s true nature.
