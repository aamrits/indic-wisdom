# Bhima

Name: *Bhīma*  
Pronunciation: *Bhee-ma*  
Meaning: *The Terrible / The Formidable One*  
Title: *Mighty son of Vayu (the wind god)*  

Bhima, the second of the five Pandavas, was born to Queen *Kunti* through the divine invocation of the *Wind god* (Vayu). 

Bhima was renowned for his immense physical strength and fierce temperament. Bhima’s character also reveals deep loyalty, emotional intensity, and a bit of mischievousness. He was both the protector of his brothers and the terror of his enemies.

---

## Birth and Lineage

Kunti, in her childhood, had served the great sage *Durvasa* with utmost reverence and honour. Satisfied with her duty and service, Durvasa gave her a mantra and said that she could obtain a son from whichever god she would invoke. 

Due to the curse of a rishi, Kunti’s husband *Pandu* couldn’t obtain a child in the normal way for fear of death. When Pandu came to know about Kunti’s boon, he wished for three sons. Kunti gave the mantra to her co-wife Madri, and she had two sons. In this way, Pandu had five sons, known as *Pandavas*.

Pandu wished his second child to have immense strength to protect the eldest son, or the to-be king. Therefore, Kunti obtained Bhima from *Vayu* with extraordinary physical might and vitality. When Bhima was just born, Kunti dropped him from her lap. Bhima fell to the mountain, but surprisingly, the stone shattered. 

---

## Major Episodes

- *Poisoning and survival*: Duryodhana attempted to poison Bhima and drown him in the Ganga. However, Bhima survived, gained even greater strength after contact with nagas, and returned more powerful than before.

- *Slayer of enemies*: Bhima killed powerful adversaries such as Hidimba, Baka, and Kicaka, protecting his brothers and Droupadi and upholding dharma. 

- *Vow against Duryodhana*: After Droupadi’s humiliation in the dice hall, Bhima vowed to break Duryodhana’s thigh and drink Duhshasana’s blood, vows he fulfilled during the war. Bhima also killed all the sons of Dhritarashtra.

- *Disobedience of orders*: Bhima once went to fetch divine flowers for Droupadi. When Hanuman advised him to pay respect to the yakshas, Bhima didn’t listen. Bhima killed Kischaka when he was in disguise of a cook. In the Mahabharata war, he rarely respected Drona or the elders and fought powerfully. After the war, Bhima never honoured Dhritarashtra and once spoke unkindly.

- *Retirement*: After hearing the death of Krishna, he, along with his brothers and Droupadi, undertook the great ascent (*Mahaprasthana*). Bhima fell last due to pride in his strength and appetite.

---

## Role in Mahabharata war

- *Bhima fulfilled his vows* that shaped the moral outcome of the war, particularly against Duhshasana and Duryodhana.

- Bhima fought and broke the chariots of powerful warriors like *Drona, Karna, Duhshasana, Duryodhana* and other kings.

- Bhima almost single-handedly brought down the powerful forces of *Kalinga*. He killed more than 10,000 elephants and most of their entire forces.

- It was Bhima’s last words that made Drona throw his weapons and sit in meditation on the battlefield, enabling Dhristadyumna to kill him. 

- When he met *Hanuman*, Bhima prayed to him to assist them in the war. He also saw the divine form of Hanuman of the *Treta Yuga*.

- *Bhima has also been restricted by a few*. Bhima couldn’t lift the tail of Hanuman with all his might. Karna had defeated Bhima. He was also restrained by *Nahusha*, in the form of a snake. He faced death like his brothers, for ignoring *Dharma* while fetching water, only to be revived by *Yuddhishthira*.

## Footnotes

- *Kunti*: Mother of Yudhishthira. The husband of Kunti is Pandu.

- *Durvasa*: A great and angry sage, often known as a part of Shiva.

- *Pandu*: The husband of Kunti, the young brother of Dhritarashtra.

- *Nahusha*: Ancient king belonging to the Lunar dynasty. Lunar dynasty started with Chandra -> Buddha -> Pururava -> Ayu -> Nahusha. Yayati was the son of Nahusha. He obtained the position of Indra for some time, but then, due to his adharma, he was cursed to become a snake by the sage Agastya. Yudhishthira freed him from the curse.

- *Drona*: The teacher of the Pandavas and Kauravas

- *Karna*: The secret son of Kunti and the eldest Pandava. However, he was the greatest friend of Duryodhana.

- *Duryodhana*: The eldest son of Dhritarashtra. Duhshasana was younger than Duryodhana.

- *Kalinga*: A powerful kingdom.

- *Hanuman*: The son of the wind-god, and that makes him the elder brother of Bhima. Hanuman was a great devotee of Rama and a powerful warrior in the Treta Yuga. In fact, he could have defeated Ravana. There are 4 yugas, Satya, Treta, Dwapara and Kali. The Mahabharata took place in the Dwapara Yuga.

- *Mahaprasthana*: After hearing of Krishna’s death, the Pandavas decided to go to heaven through the Himalayas. Their ascent is known as Mahaprasthana.

- *Dhristadyumna*: The son of Drupada, born from a sacrifice, especially to slay Drona.
